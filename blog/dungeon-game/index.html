Dungeon Game

LeetCode #174


Description

The demons had captured the princess (P) and imprisoned her in the bottom-right corner of a dungeon. The dungeon consists of M x N rooms laid out in a 2D grid. Our valiant knight (K) was initially positioned in the top-left room and must fight his way through the dungeon to rescue the princess.

The knight has an initial health point represented by a positive integer. If at any point his health point drops to 0 or below, he dies immediately.

Some of the rooms are guarded by demons, so the knight loses health (negative integers) upon entering these rooms; other rooms are either empty (0’s) or contain magic orbs that increase the knight’s health (positive integers).

In order to reach the princess as quickly as possible, the knight decides to move only rightward or downward in each step.

Write a function to determine the knight’s minimum initial health so that he is able to rescue the princess.

For example, given the dungeon below, the initial health of the knight must be at least 7 if he follows the optimal path RIGHT-> RIGHT -> DOWN -> DOWN.

Notes:

  • The knight’s health has no upper bound.
  • Any room can contain threats or power-ups, even the first room the knight enters and the bottom-right room where the princess is imprisoned.


My Thoughts

This is a dynamic programming problem, so I have to think about the table first. MinHealth[i][j] is the minimum health of the knight should have if he wants to go from (i,j) to (n,n). But I will build the table from (n,n) to (0,0) instead of (0,0) to (n,n).

And for minHealth[i][j], the value of it may be minimum of minHealth[i][j+1] (from down) or minHealth[i+1][j] (from right) - dungeon[i][j]. If the value is smaller than 0, then it should be 1. Let me explain how does this solution come from:

  1. If at (i,j), knight will lose hp, it means that after the knight’s hp - dungeon[i][j], his hp should still larger than or equal to minHealth at either (i+1,j) or (i,j+1). Otherwise, he cannot go to (n,n). Therefore, I choose the miniimum value of minHealth[i+1][j] or minHealth[i][j+1].
  2. If at (i,j), knight will get some buff to increase his hp, it means that after the knight’s hp + dungeon[i][j], his hp should still larger than or equal to minHealth at either (i+1,j) or (i,j+1). However, in some cases, dungeon[i][j] may much larger than minHealth he needs, then he just needs 1 hp when he enters this room. (His hp cannot be less than 1!)
  3. After handling normal cases, there are some corner cases at right most rooms and down most rooms. I can skip some overflow rooms.

After finishing the table, just return minHealth at (0,0).


Solution

class Solution(object):
    def calculateMinimumHP(self, dungeon):
        """
        :type dungeon: List[List[int]]
        :rtype: int
        """
        if len(dungeon) == 0:
            return 1
        
        minHealth = []
        for i in range(0, len(dungeon)):
            l = []
            for j in range(0, len(dungeon[i])):
                l.append(None)
            minHealth.append(l)
        
        for i in range(len(dungeon)-1, -1, -1):
            for j in range(len(dungeon[i])-1, -1, -1):
                if i == len(dungeon)-1 and j == len(dungeon[i])-1:
                    minHealth[i][j] = max(1, 1 - dungeon[i][j])
                elif i == len(dungeon)-1:
                    minHealth[i][j] = max(1, minHealth[i][j+1] - dungeon[i][j])
                elif j == len(dungeon[i])-1:
                    minHealth[i][j] = max(1, minHealth[i+1][j] - dungeon[i][j])
                else:
                    minHealth[i][j] = max(1, min(minHealth[i][j+1], minHealth[i+1][j]) - dungeon[i][j])
        return minHealth[0][0]
